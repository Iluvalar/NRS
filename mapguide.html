Iluvalar's map guide for warzone2100 competitive scene

Truck time around 45-80s

This is the time it take to send a truck from one base to the other. The exact number of tiles this represent vary depending on the terrain type.
The defender have time to produce more units or produce new components that the attacker do not have access to at the moment he send his attack. The attacker needs an avantage (better unit composition, better research, higher power...) in order for his attack to be succesful. The bigger the distance, the more the attacker needs to wait and build up unit before his attack. Some of our tech such as machine gun, are time sensitive. And require the attacker to somewhat "rush" the enemy before he gets better components. Machine guns are assumed to be availabe during balance, and therefore the distance between bases must stay short enough.

Too short distances cause another type of problem: players are facing a blind choice at the start of the game. Our weapon modifiers makes some starting weapons stronger against other weapons and propulsion. The defender advantage must be greater than the blind weapon modifier advantage. or game will collapse and be lost out of shear luck of the draw.

On a techical note, I want to point out the existance of the MG tower rush strategy that becomes extremely strong if the distance between players gets too small. This completely change the nature of the game, build order, etc... Many "mini" maps have been made over the years, they are a lot of fun, but ultimately a completely different game

21 tile per oil fraction rule

Choke points in the middle of the map, should be about 21 tile wide in total. In FFA, it's possible to control 80% of your oil behind a 16 tile chokepoint at least for the short term. This is what I mean by "oil fraction"
Early maps, such as startup and rush turns out to have 21 tile frontier. This informed the structure balance to have about 3x more hp than units. This is 3x more hp against units of ~7 unit range = 21 tiles. It became the standard and will directly affect how defensive structure perform. Small choke point allows turtling and bigger open maps makes the structures worthless. Structures are expected to be a defenssive option agaisnt flamers who are notiously bad against concrete. We need structure for balance, and therefore the 21 tile rule have to be enforced withing tolerance of course.

Good oil placement

No dead ends, no oil hidden in a corner or in the middle of a large field. Unless they are marked (8p-ziggurat style).
Players might not be familliar with all the maps and it's already a huge advantage for those who knows. We should have a reasonnable trust that the mapmaker didn't hid oil derricks or oil drum in hard to spot locations.

No T3 defences

Players should expect some game to last for an hour, but the map should not force them to research 30 minutes before the game can start. Huge T3 defences or large scavengers can inflate the time of the game, they should be avoided. One player could have better research skill and after a 30 minutes of simcity gameplay, steam roll the opponent. Neither of the 2 players will find this particulalry fun.

One main path

At no point, out of random choices should a player find his army closer to both bases than it is to the enemy units

Uniform terrain type

Smaller pet peve but the A* algorithm does not account for terrain speed. If the shortest route is on particularly bad terrain, it will require a lot of micro-management to get the units to destination in the best time. The map should not trap players who just want to get from point A to point B into deep mud when a far better route exists.
